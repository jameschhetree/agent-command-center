Keywords you should recognize

Healthcare AI
Ambient scribe
AI that records doctor-patient visits and drafts the clinical note. Abridge, Nuance DAX, Suki are the names. KP's large deployment was led by Ainsley.
SaMD Software as a Medical Device
FDA's regulatory pathway for software that functions as a medical device. Matters for any clinical-decision AI that touches diagnosis or treatment.
Red Zone AI Agent™
Ainsley's coined framework — the last 20 yards of AI deployment where strategy meets clinical workflow reality. Use this term when her thesis comes up.
RAG Retrieval-Augmented Generation
LLMs grounded in source docs rather than pure generation. The primary way healthcare AI reduces hallucination risk.
PHI Protected Health Information
The data HIPAA governs. The biggest friction point for healthcare AI training and deployment. Know this cold.
NEMT · Kinetik's World
NEMT Non-Emergency Medical Transportation
Medicaid-covered rides to dialysis, doctor visits, pharmacy. Billions in federal spend. Kinetik sits in the data/payments layer.
Broker model
Modivcare, MTM, Access2Care contract with state Medicaid plans (MCOs), then dispatch to transportation providers. Legacy model Kinetik modernizes.
MCO Managed Care Organization
The private insurer running a state's Medicaid plan. Anthem, Humana, Centene, Molina. They're who Kinetik ultimately serves.
Tier rates
Payment tiers by ride type: ambulatory → wheelchair van (WAV) → stretcher. Each tier has different unit economics. WAV is where the margin math gets interesting.
Fraud enforcement
Live issue. Fabricated or overbilled trips. 2024–25 saw ramped-up state and federal enforcement, which reshaped the competitive landscape.
Policy · Rural Health
1115 waiver
A state's permission slip from CMS to experiment with Medicaid rules. Every big state reform uses one.
340B
Federal program forcing drug-maker discounts for safety-net hospitals. Politically charged; rural hospitals depend on it.
REH Rural Emergency Hospital
2023 CMS designation — a small hospital keeps its ER and outpatient, sheds inpatient, gets enhanced Medicare reimbursement. A real option for some of Beshear's 35.
CAH Critical Access Hospital
Rural hospital with under 25 beds, reimbursed on a cost-plus basis by Medicare. The traditional rural survival mechanism.
Medicaid unwinding
The post-COVID redetermination wave that kicked millions off Medicaid rolls in 2023–24. Still reverberating in MCO enrollment.
CMMI
Center for Medicare & Medicaid Innovation. Runs value-based care pilots. Where most "new model" experimentation lives.
Political · Who Runs What
ITT In This Together PAC
Beshear's leadership PAC. Raised $4.3M in 2025. Super PAC + 501(c)(4) combo. Widely read as presidential prep.
DGA Democratic Governors Association
The party's governor-recruitment and support org. Beshear is 2026 chair — a platform-maker for the national stage.
Eric Hyers
Beshear's longtime strategist. Ran both gubernatorial campaigns and runs ITT now. Not on the host committee, but the name to know if it comes up.
The 2028 field
Beshear, Newsom (CA), Shapiro (PA), Pritzker (IL), Whitmer (MI), Klobuchar (MN) — names most mentioned. Beshear's lane is "red-state moderate who wins."
Public affairs ≠ lobbying
Lobbying directly asks lawmakers for outcomes. Public affairs builds the coalitions and narratives that make those asks land. Baldick's HPS and Ainsley Health both live in public affairs.

Beshear in 90 seconds

The man. Andy Beshear, 48. Son of former KY Gov. Steve Beshear. Former KY Attorney General. Won governor 2019 by 0.4%, reelected 2023 by 5% against Trump-endorsed Daniel Cameron in a state Trump won by 26.

The brand. Red-state Democrat. Faith-forward. Crisis-tested (2021 tornadoes, 2022 floods, COVID). Signed Medicaid expansion protections. Blocked school choice legislation. Restored voting rights to 180k Kentuckians with nonviolent felonies.

The play. 2028 is the subtext of every Beshear event right now. ITT PAC is the fundraising shell. DGA chairmanship is the national stage. His through-line is "empathy + competence" — positioning as the anti-chaos candidate.

The policy drumbeat. Rural healthcare — 35 KY hospitals at risk of closure, which he blames on tariffs and federal cuts. This is the live wire in the room.

III. Strategic framing

This is a Beshear-for-2028 positioning event. His PAC raised $4.3M in 2025 and everyone in the room knows what that means.

You're arriving with Ainsley, Sufian, and Dennis Cardoza — clinical AI operator, NEMT infrastructure CEO, and top DC healthcare lobbyist. That's not a BDR walking in cold. That's a real team with real credibility.

The opening is that Beshear's loudest policy drumbeat is rural healthcare access — the 35 KY hospitals at risk. That lands on the exact intersection your team occupies. Use it.

IV. Your pitch, tight
"We help health systems and investors figure out what's real in healthcare AI versus what's hype. Dr. MacLean led AI deployment at Kaiser's Mid-Atlantic region — we package that perspective through a podcast, a newsletter, and advisory work."

Humble frame when you need it:

"I came out of a sales background and I'm relatively new to healthcare — been going deep on NEMT economics and doing the Stanford AI in Healthcare cert."
V. Do not do this
  • Don't pitch Beshear or his staff on anything. Not that event.
  • Don't raise ARC, addiction treatment reimbursement, or the FBI investigation with anyone. That topic is radioactive.
  • Don't hand out cards aggressively. 4–6 good conversations beats 15 shallow ones.
  • Don't oversell your role. "I work closely with Dr. MacLean on GTM and content" is cleaner than overstating the stake.
  • Don't fake healthcare SME. Stated learning curves earn respect. Performed expertise doesn't.
